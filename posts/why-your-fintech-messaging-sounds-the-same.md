# Why Your Fintech's Messaging Sounds Like Everyone Else's

Go look at five fintech homepages right now. I'll wait.

Back? Did you see it? Secure. Seamless. Scalable. Maybe "built for the modern CFO" or "the all-in-one platform finance teams love." Maybe a hero image of a dashboard nobody uses in real life.

This is not a coincidence. It's a category trap — and it's killing your conversion rate.

## How the Category Trap Works

When a new category emerges, early movers define the language. Everyone who enters afterward copies that language because it "sounds right for the space." The problem is that category language describes the category, not your company. It gives prospects zero reason to choose you over anyone else.

Fintech is particularly prone to this because the technical differentiators are genuinely hard to communicate. "We use ML to optimize working capital" sounds impressive to your engineering team and means nothing to the CFO you're trying to sell.

## The Three Rewrites That Actually Work

**Rewrite 1: Swap features for outcomes at the customer level.**

Before: "Real-time cash flow visibility across all accounts."
After: "Know by 9am whether you can make payroll on Friday."

The second version creates a scene. The buyer sees themselves in it.

**Rewrite 2: Name the villain.**

Your product solves a problem. That problem has a cause. Name it. "Most finance teams are still reconciling in spreadsheets" is a villain. "Manual, error-prone processes" is a cliché. Be specific about what's broken and why.

**Rewrite 3: Make a claim your competitors can't copy.**

"We're the only platform that [specific thing]" is infinitely more compelling than "we're the best." If you can't finish that sentence, that's your positioning problem — not your copywriting problem.

## The Real Question

Why does your fintech exist? Not what does it do — why does it exist? What would be worse in the world if you shut down tomorrow?

If you can answer that in one sentence, your homepage almost writes itself. If you can't, no amount of polished design or clever copy will fix your conversion rate.

Start there.
